Dairy Sector

The dairy industry has been around for centuries; nevertheless, it has undergone several transformations throughout history. In ancient times, cheese was made from curdled milk or from the curdled whey left after churning butter. In Greco-Roman era, cheese was sold in pottery jars to avoid contamination from bacteria. Later on, coke (refined) milk was produced by separating the whole milk into skimmed and concentrated forms. During this period of transformations, the dairy industry became complicated as it required different skills and skill sets which were acquired through education or professional experience. In addition to that, there are different stages in the production process such as animal breeding, milking, processing, packaging and distribution of dairy products worldwide. Furthermore, there are different types of dairy products such as fresh or processed milk which include yogurt, butter or cheese among others.



The dairy industry is a major contributor to the economy in many countries including Australia, Canada, China and the United States where there are over 200 million cows producing 27 billion gallons of milk annually1 . In the US alone over $22 billion is spent yearly on dairy products2 . According to experts surveyed by Agri-Food Canada on trends in the Canadian dairy industry from 2016 to 20223 , demand for Canadian dairy products is increasing due to population growth and economic development4 . With this increase in demand for Canadian dairy products also comes increase in health standards for these products such as pasteurization— which kills bacteria — cooling systems — which reduces bacterial growth — probiotics — which add beneficial bacteria to produce yogurt5 . There are two main types of pasteurization processes used today: thermal (heat) pasteurization at 118°F (48°C) for 15 seconds followed by holding at 131°F (55°C) for 15 seconds; and enzymatic (yeast) pasteurization at 162°F (72°C) for 30 seconds followed by holding at 180F (82C) for 2 minutes6 .



Global demand for milk and milk products has increased due to modern technology such as refrigeration systems that keep perishable foods cold without using ice— thus reducing energy consumption7 . Furthermore, global agricultural growth has increased global demand even more8 since people with an adequate income can purchase quality foodstuff such as dairy products9 . Urbanization has also contributed to increased demand since cities have limited space available for storing foodstuff10 . Therefore urban residents rely more on packaged foodstuff that requires minimal storage compared with rural residents who have less access to these types of foodstuffs11 . For example: city dwellers can purchase fresh meat daily compared with rural dwellers who may only eat meat once a week or less12 .

Who We Are

Milk is an important part of many people’s diets, but can be a controversial one. In addition to being a major part of the food industry and public health, milk comes from animals that are milked by mammals. This is how the milk industry functions. The milk is extracted from cows and goats and processed into dairy products. Even though most people know that milk comes from cows, it can be difficult to understand the complex nature of this industry.



The dairy industry is under pressure from consumers to eat less or healthier versions of products containing milk proteins. This has led to constant scrutiny of the dairy industry by governments, non-profit organizations and media outlets. For example, in 2015 the UK’s National Health Service launched a campaign against high-fat dairy products called “Don’t Play with your Food” because it promotes eating less fat. The goal of this campaign is to encourage people to cut back on high-fat dairy products like cheese and ice cream and choose lower-fat versions instead. In response, dairy companies work closely with governments to promote good nutritional habits while limiting health warnings about eating too much dairy products.
